Brain Excerpt

## Core Thesis

Capitalist macro systems are unstable because the prices and quantities that matter most are set by forward-looking financial decisions made under fundamental uncertainty. In the open economy, exchange rates are driven by portfolio capital flows and shared mental models, not by trade flows or purchasing power parity; the actual exchange rate can diverge from the balanced-trade exchange rate indefinitely. In the domestic economy, investment is the unstable driver of the cycle: animal spirits lift expected profits, investment expands capacity and debt commitments, realized profits eventually disappoint, and the boom turns into bust. Crises emerge when optimistic forecasts outrun the real and financial structures capable of validating them.

---

## Axioms

| ID | Axiom | Therefore... |
|----|-------|-------------|
| A1 | Portfolio capital flows dominate exchange-rate determination | Start FX analysis with expected financial returns and capital allocation, not the current account |
| A2 | The actual exchange rate is not pulled toward the balanced-trade exchange rate | Persistent trade imbalances are normal; BTER is diagnostic, not an attractor |
| A3 | Expectations are causal market inputs | Forecast formation must be modeled directly rather than treated as noise |
| A4 | Agents forecast through shared mental models under uncertainty | News matters through interpretation, attractors, heuristics, and confidence |
| A5 | Bandwagons are endogenous to FX | Price action can create the flows that validate the trend until confidence breaks |
| A6 | UIP fails because confidence is incomplete | Return gaps can persist without requiring a stable risk-premium story |
| A7 | Trade imbalances require trade finance, not necessarily speculative portfolio inflows | Split capital flows into trade-finance capital and speculative/portfolio capital |
| A8 | Absolute advantage can drive persistent trade imbalances | Cost, technology, and income differences need not self-correct through prices |
| A9 | Currency crises build at three tension points | Track currency-forecast divergence, financial-return divergence, and financial fragility |
| A10 | Financial fragility rises during good times | Easy repayment lowers margins of safety and shifts units from hedge to speculative/Ponzi finance |
| A11 | Investment is the unstable driver of the business cycle | Recessions are generated endogenously by the investment-capital-profit loop, even when policy or shocks contribute |
| A12 | Crises are forecast-validation failures | The shock is when agents realize expectations are out of line with what the system can deliver |

Crisis Module Excerpt

## Core Claim

Crises are systemic. They are not random shocks hitting an otherwise stable economy. The common root is the development of increasingly optimistic expectations alongside real and financial structures that cannot validate those expectations. In open economies, Harvey locates the break in one or more of three tension points.

```

│                  HARVEY CRISIS ARCHITECTURE                          │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  TP1: Currency-forecast divergence                                   │
│       AER moves away from the mental-model shadow value               │
│                                                                      │
│  TP2: Financial-returns divergence                                   │
│       Financial asset returns outrun real investment returns          │

│  TP3: Financial fragility                                            │
```
