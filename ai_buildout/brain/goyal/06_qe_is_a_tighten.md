# 06 · QE is a Tighten

> CORE CLAIM: QE swaps a risky asset (a Treasury) for a riskless one (reserves),
> REDUCING the aggregate income and risk in private hands. It is not money
> printing — it is recomposition. Its first- and second-order mechanics are
> deflationary. It "works" only through psychology / signaling. Counter-cyclical
> policy must do the OPPOSITE of the market: sell safe, buy risky.

Source: [6-qe-deflationary.md](../../converted/6-qe-deflationary.md)

---

## What QE actually is

```
        FED                          BANK
   ┌────────────┬──────────┐    ┌──────────┬──────────┐
   │ +Treasury  │ +Reserves│    │ -Treasury│          │
   └────────────┴──────────┘    │ +Reserves│          │
                                 └──────────┴──────────┘
   The Fed CANNOT buy from non-banks directly (it can't create deposits, only
   reserves). "Printing" = creating value from nothing. QE creates NOTHING new —
   it RECOMPOSES money from Treasuries (great collateral) into reserves (poor
   collateral, ch.03). It removes the Treasury from the private sector.
```

## First-order effect: less income, less risk

```
   risky asset (Treasury, positive yield)  ──►  riskless asset (reserve)
   aggregate income from the asset set  ↓        aggregate risk in the system ↓
   (unless the curve is inverted — Fed almost never buys the low-yielder)
   ⇒ weight moves FLOW → STOCK on the seesaw (ch.05) = DEFLATIONARY
```

## Second-order effects: also deflationary

```
   QE bids up the REMAINING assets:                QE discourages NEW assets:
   ─────────────────────────────────             ──────────────────────────────
   1 future income of those assets ↓             1 less collateral to finance
   2 fewer participants can hold them              creation (bonds → poor reserves)
     (risky asset supply ↓ ⇒ fewer share          2 less income & productivity from
      the "wealth effect")                           new assets
   3 negative wealth effect for everyone          3 skews creation from LOANS
     SHORT assets (everyone w/o assets)              (inflationary) to securities
                                                     (less so) by killing what
                                                     bonds diversify against
                                                   4 raises cost of capital for
                                                     "bond-unlike" borrowers
```

Netting first + second order: **the most likely mechanical outcome of QE is a
REDUCTION in inflationary potential.** The QE era ran LOWER average inflation
than the pre-QE era (across 6 countries' experiences).

## The only channel left: psychology ("psych ops")

```
   intended QE channels (BoE / Liberty St.):
     1 lower long rates / term premia ─┐
     3 capital gains → wealth          ├─ all DEFLATIONARY here
     4 banks lend more (reserves)     ─┘
     2 SIGNAL the policy stance        ◄── the ONLY one that can stimulate
                                            (sentiment / expectations / shock therapy)

   FOMC in their own words:
     Lockhart: "Will it work in practice, or are we just dealing with PSYCH OPS?"
     Yellen:   "no point saving ammunition if the ammunition loses its potency"
     Dudley:   "very important... we don't signal that we're out of ammunition"
   ⇒ for QE to "work," people must react over-optimistically. It works iff
     enough people believe it does, because it doesn't work in theory.
```

## The insurance lens (why QE makes the system MORE at risk)

```
   bonds = INSURANCE on the economy. QE buys up insurance ⇒ insurance EXPENSIVE.
   When your insurance gets expensive you either:
       buy less of the thing insured   OR   carry it under-insured
   Both are worse outcomes.
   ⇒ QE doesn't make a system that NEEDS no insurance; it makes one that CAN'T
     AFFORD insurance. Buying insurance = SHORTING the underlying (the economy).
   ⇒ QE = the Fed going SHORT the economy.  QT = the Fed going LONG the economy.
     Policy is only stimulative if it's LONG the economy.
```

## The counter-cyclical test, and the exception that proves it

```
   counter-cyclical = do the OPPOSITE of what the market is already doing.
   In a slump the market FLEES to safe assets ⇒ the Fed should SELL safe / BUY risky.
   QE does the opposite of counter-cyclical: it buys what the market already wants.

   EXCEPTION — buying UNDESIRABLE assets IS stimulative (adds a buyer to a market
   that has none, at a premium to the would-be price):
        • QE1 purchase of distressed MBS (2009)
        • long-duration Treasury buying (second half of March 2020)
        • the Bank Term Funding Program (BTFP, 2023)
```

## No free lunch except diversification

```
   the Fed cannot raise AGGREGATE return or lower AGGREGATE risk — it only shifts
   WHO bears the burden and who gets the tailwind. Its one genuine power is to
   provide DIVERSIFICATION — and that requires a HIGH risk-free yield, so actors
   can move out the risk curve INSURED. (Fully developed in ch.07.)
```
