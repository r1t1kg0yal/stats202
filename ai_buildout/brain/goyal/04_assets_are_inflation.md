# 04 · Assets ARE Inflation

> CORE CLAIM: Money is bank balance-sheet space. The system's inflationary
> potential is its money-creators' capacity (and willingness) to expand balance
> sheets. Money is created across THREE balance sheets — public, private
> observable, and private UNOBSERVABLE — and the last is the biggest and the
> one that imploded in 2008.

## Money = balance-sheet space

```
        BANK                                    BORROWER
   ┌──────────┬───────────┐               ┌──────────┬───────────┐
   │ +Loan 6% │ +Deposit 3%│              │ +Deposit │ +Loan      │
   └──────────┴───────────┘               └──────────┴───────────┘
   No cash. No reserves. An accounting identity creates the money and a 3% margin.

   The bank's daily game:
     constraint 1: assets = liabilities + equity
     constraint 2: enough equity (CET1) + reserves (LCR) to satisfy regulation
     objective:    maximize  (total assets) × (avg interest margin)

   Determinants of balance-sheet capacity:
     DEMAND side : willing & able borrowers
     SUPPLY side : cost of funding · perception of borrower credit risk
```

## The liability-management revolution (1950s–70s)

```
   PRE-liability-management          LIABILITY-MANAGEMENT ERA (wholesale finance)
   ────────────────────────         ───────────────────────────────────────────
   funded only by DEPOSITS           fund the asset book with NON-deposit money:
   (asset side managed only)         commercial paper, repo, Fed funds, EURODOLLARS
                                     ⇒ banks manage BOTH sides of the b/s
                                     ⇒ expansion far beyond core deposits
                                     ⇒ "stitch" high-yield assets to cheap liabilities
   This matured in the 1960s–70s and is integral to the Great Inflation.
```

### Why unobservable money matters: the hidden state

```
   the 2009 DOLLAR SPIKE puzzle:
     widening US current-account deficit "should" have WEAKENED the dollar...
     ...but the dollar SPIKED in 2009.
   WHY? offshore-offshore dollar portfolios died in the shadows → shadow banks
        had to BID for dollars to repay dollar borrowings. Money was destroyed
        where no balance-of-payments model could see it.

   ⇒ market PRICES are the only window into the monetary shadows. The SAME
     observable signal can mean OPPOSITE things depending on what the
     unobservable leg is doing.
```

```
   Eurodollar mechanics (money created entirely outside the US / Fed):
     P1 moves a $100 deposit offshore → London bank now has $100 of funding →
     it lends $100 to P2 → P2 banks in the US, recreating a $100 US deposit.
     Domestic US balance sheet: UNCHANGED. Global dollars: +$100. M1/M2: flat.
```

## Observable growth is often evidence of unobservable contraction

```
   "Who will buy all the debt?" (bond vigilantism) FAILED 2011–2020:
     when private unobservable creation is weak, it is a SELLER'S market for
     new safe assets. Government bonds ARE collateral; when collateral is scarce,
     gov't bond yields stay LOW even as issuance soars. Vigilantism was asked at
     the wrong time.
```

## The 2008 break and the post-cyclical paradigm

```
   every series that proxies the unobservable leg looks the SAME:
      exponential growth ──► 2008 ──► flat plateau ever since (trillions short)

   ┌─────────────────────────── REGIME SHIFT ───────────────────────────┐
   │  CYCLICAL (1950–80s)            POST-CYCLICAL (2010s)                │
   │  jet fuel: credit booms & busts  frying oil: sputters along ≈        │
   │  banks take risk → money → cycles  long-run productivity             │
   │                                  no boom (nothing to boom),          │
   │                                  no bust (nothing to bust)           │
   │  alpha (timing the cycle) wins   beta (just hold scarce assets) wins │
   └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
   "Strong banks" (Powell's praise) is the PROBLEM — strong = less risk-taking
   = less money. A banking paradox of thrift.
```

## Risk crowds IN risk (the MMT crowd-in seed)

```
   PUBLIC deficit ──► PRIVATE surplus ──► system equity ↑ ──► more private
   (one's borrowing = another's collateral: income or an asset to hold)   borrowing

   ⇒ deficit expansion can drag the system OUT of the post-cyclical paradigm.
     "Risk doesn't crowd OUT risk, it crowds IN risk." (developed fully in ch.05/07)
```

## The fiscal lever and fiscal dominance

```
   the right question isn't "what rate gives 2% inflation" but
   "what DEFICIT gives 2% inflation" — and we can't know, because we can't
   measure unobservable money. The SAME 2% inflation occurred at deficits of
   -1% (1955), 3% (1987), 8% (2011), 12% (2021) of GDP.

   POST-COVID = FISCAL DOMINANCE: public borrowing becomes the PRIMARY driver
   of the cycle, private borrowing secondary. In that regime, the Fed's tools
   can flip: raising rates may add MORE public money creation than it subtracts
   private. (→ ch.07)
```
