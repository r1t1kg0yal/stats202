# 08 · The Myth of Volcker

> CORE CLAIM: Volcker did not defeat inflation by holding rates high. Before 1981
> the Fed exerted almost no control over the funds rate — it floated, describing
> CONDITIONS. Volcker's real move was to REPURPOSE interest rates into a SIGNAL
> of policy stance, swapping failed mechanics for psychology. He is the greatest
> central banker precisely because he convinced the world the Fed mattered.

Source: [8-myth-of-volcker.md](../../converted/8-myth-of-volcker.md)

## The headline inversion

```
  ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ Credit Volcker not with DEFEATING the 1981 inflation, but with        │
  │ CAPITALIZING on the 1981 recession (which he did not cause) to impose  │
  │ a new policymaking paradigm.                                          │
  │                                                                       │
  │ To credit him with beating inflation in 1981, you must ALSO credit    │
  │ him with the Global Safe-Asset Shortage (2002–06) and the GFC (2007–08).│
  └────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Pre-1981: the Fed funds rate FLOATED (it wasn't a lever)

```
   1970s MONETARIST model:
     set reserve target + a funds-rate RANGE
            │
            ▼  supply reserves to the banking system
       ┌────┴─────────────────────────────┐
       │ funds rate stays inside the band  │ → "the rate has been FLOATING"
       │ funds rate hits a boundary        │ → hold a consultation → CHANGE the band
       └───────────────────────────────────┘   (it floated again)

   Volcker (May 1980): "since October it has not made any difference what the
   federal funds range was... every time we've had a consultation we have in fact
   CHANGED the range."
   Volcker (Feb 1981): "did we ever have a consultation and NOT change it? I don't
   think so."
```

## The mechanics simply didn't exist

```
   reserve targeting ──X──► monetary aggregates ──X──► economic activity
                  (Eurodollar creation circumvented reserves entirely)

   Volcker (May 1980): "I don't know whether $1 billion of excess reserves and a
   13% discount rate will give us a funds rate of 7% or zero. We really are in an
   area of UNKNOWN RELATIONSHIPS."
   Greenspan (2000): "inflation has to be a monetary phenomenon... but [policy on
   money] presupposes we can LOCATE money. That has become a dubious proposition."

   Themes admitted across 1979–82 FOMC transcripts:
     1 we do not understand the reserve ↔ aggregate ↔ funds-rate ↔ activity links
     2 CREDIBILITY fears constantly overshadowed economic outcomes
     3 a funds-rate-level → slowdown linkage was NEVER isolated
```

## 1981: the repurposing — rates become a SIGNAL

```
   OLD: rates are the OUTCOME of reserve management (a description of conditions)
   NEW: rates are the THING WE CONTROL — used to SIGNAL stance, with the economy
        expected to fulfill the prophecy on the Fed's behalf.

   Volcker (Feb 1981): "father isn't telling them" — by refusing to own the rate,
   the Fed left markets guessing; assert authority and the long market follows.
   Corrigan (May 1981): "the funds rate... is the only thing we have in the short
   run that is fairly UNAMBIGUOUS." → and thus the Fed-wording-watchers complex was born.
```

```
   ┌────────────────── THE NEW GAME: RED-LIGHT / GREEN-LIGHT ──────────────────┐
   │  the Fed says "tighter" / "easier";  the economy is asked to ACT it out.   │
   │  it worked 1981–~2013 because a healthy global dollar system handled the    │
   │  MECHANICS while the Fed's communications nudged PSYCHOLOGY.                 │
   └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## So what actually caused the 1981–82 recession?

```
   NOT a managed contraction in lending. Volcker's hikes were so INFLATIONARY
   that they overdrove the system's non-inflation-adjusted stabilizers:

     • TAX BRACKET CREEP        wages pushed into higher (fixed) brackets → real
                                fiscal tightening
     • REAL fiscal contraction  high inflation shrank REAL spending & REAL debt (MMT)
     • BANK-CAPITAL RULES       US bank books aren't inflation-adjusted; double-digit
                                inflation ERODED real capital → banks breached lending
                                limits → abruptly cut lending to EM
                                ──► the LATIN AMERICAN DEBT CRISIS (a collateral
                                    contraction ~ GFC-scale in the rest-of-world's
                                    dollar assets) ──► spilled into the US economy

   the FOMC never attributed the slowdown to the hikes; as late as May 1981 it
   FORECAST continued strength. Recession arrived through a banking-regulation
   fluke, not a managed lever.
```

## The two-sentence epitaph

```
  ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │   Prior to 1981, the Fed did not exist.                            │
  │   Since 2013, the Fed does not exist.                              │
  │                                                                    │
  │   (1981–2013 = the window in which the global dollar system let     │
  │    the Fed's APPEARANCE of control substitute for real mechanics.)  │
  └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

   Volcker is the greatest central banker of all time because he convinced the
   world the Fed mattered — and that appearance is the primary channel through
   which a central bank exerts any control at all.
```
