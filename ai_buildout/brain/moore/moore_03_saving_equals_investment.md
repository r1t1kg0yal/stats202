```

╠═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╣

║    → Abstain from consumption                                ║
║    → Accumulate wealth                                       ║
║    → VOLITIONAL action                                       ║
║                                                               ║
║  "To save" = transitive verb                                 ║
║  "I save FOR something"                                      ║
║                                                               ║
╚═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝

╔═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║  AGGREGATE LEVEL (Macro)                                     ║
╠═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╣
║                                                               ║
║  Total Saving = Total Investment                             ║
║    → ACCOUNTING IDENTITY                                     ║
║    → Always true, by definition                              ║
║    → Mostly NON-volitional                                   ║
║                                                               ║

║  "Income not consumed" = "Investment"                        ║
║                                                               ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝

    ERROR: Assuming macro S = Σ(individual volitional saving)
    
    TRUTH: Macro S = Accounting record of I

## The Two Identities

```
    IDENTITY 1: Saving = Income not consumed
    ─────────
    
        Y ≡ C + I     (output definition)
        Y ≡ C + S     (income allocation)
        
        ∴ S ≡ Y - C
        
        
    IDENTITY 2: Saving = Investment
    ─────────
    
        Y ≡ C + I     (output)
        Y ≡ C + S     (income)
        
        ∴ S ≡ I
        
        
    CRITICAL INSIGHT:
    ────────────────
    
    These hold ALWAYS:
      • At every moment in time
      • At every level of income
      • Whether economy is "in equilibrium" or not
      • Whether interest rates are "right" or "wrong"
      
    S ≡ I is NOT an equilibrium condition!
    It's an ACCOUNTING TAUTOLOGY
```

## Volitional vs Non-Volitional Saving

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ VOLITIONAL SAVING (Small fraction of total)                │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                             │
│ 1. Households abstain from consumption                     │
│    → Purchase newly issued bonds/stocks                     │
│    → Directly finance someone else's investment            │
│                                                             │
│ 2. Small firms retain earnings                             │
│    → Owner-operators decide to invest                      │
│    → Internal finance of their own investment              │
│                                                             │
│ Share of total: ~10-20% of total saving                   │
│                                                             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ NON-VOLITIONAL SAVING (Majority of total)                  │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                             │
│ 1. CONVENIENCE LENDING                                      │
│    Firms borrow from banks → New deposits created          │
│    Depositors "do nothing" → Accumulate deposits           │
│    = Non-volitional saving                                  │
│                                                             │
│ 2. CAPITAL GAINS (retained earnings)                       │
│    Corporations retain earnings → Share prices rise        │
│    Shareholders "do nothing" → Wealth increases            │
│    = Non-volitional saving                                  │
│                                                             │
│ Share of total: ~80-90% of total saving                   │
│                                                             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

    KEY: Total saving is MOSTLY passive accumulation
         NOT active "abstention from consumption"
```

## The Paradox of Thrift (Reinterpreted)

```
    MAINSTREAM VIEW                  POST-KEYNESIAN VIEW
    ═══════════════                  ═══════════════════
    
    Households decide               Households decide
    to save more                    to save more
         │                               │
         │ Abstain from                  │ Abstain from
         │ consumption                   │ consumption
         ↓                               ↓
    +Supply of loanable            -Consumption spending
     funds                              │
         │                               │ AD falls
         ↓                               ↓
    Interest rate falls            -Expected future AD
         │                               │
         ↓                               ↓
    +Investment                    -Investment
         │                               │
         ↓                               ↓
    +Actual saving                 -Actual saving
    
    (Saving causes I↑)             (Saving causes I↓)
    
    
    MOORE'S POINT:
    
        Volitional saving = Reduction in C
        
        Reduction in C = Reduction in AD
        
        Reduction in AD → Expectations of lower future AD
        
        Lower expected AD → Less investment
        
        Less investment = Less actual saving (S ≡ I)
        
        ∴ Attempt to save more → Actually save LESS
           (Paradox of thrift)
```

## Investment Creates Its Own Saving

```
    THE CAUSAL SEQUENCE
    ═══════════════════
    
    t=0: Firm decides to invest $100 in new machine
         (Volitional investment decision)
         
    t=1: Firm borrows $100 from bank
         ├─ +$100 Loan (bank asset)
         └─ +$100 Deposit (bank liability)
         
    t=2: Firm buys machine, pays supplier
         ├─ Supplier receives $100 deposit
         └─ Supplier does NOTHING (convenience lending)
         
    t=3: Accounting:
         ├─ Investment = $100 (machine purchased)
         └─ Saving = $100 (deposits accumulated)
         
    WHO "SAVED"?
    ────────────
    • Supplier: passively accumulated deposits ($100)
    • Did NOT decide to "abstain from consumption"
    • Did NOT decide to "provide funds for investment"
    • Simply ACCEPTED money in exchange for machine
    
    = NON-VOLITIONAL SAVING
    
    
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │                                                      │
    │  Investment CREATES saving                          │
    │  Not: Saving enables investment                     │
    │                                                      │
    │  Direction: I → S, not S → I                        │
    │                                                      │
    └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## The Mechanism: How I Creates S

```
    INTERNAL FINANCE (≈50% of investment)
    ════════════════════════════════════
    
    Corporation retains $50 earnings (doesn't pay dividend)
         │
         ↓
    Invests in new capital
         │
         ↓
    Shareholders' equity value rises $50
         │
         ↓
    Shareholders do NOT consume capital gains
         │
         ↓
    = Saving of $50 (non-volitional)
    
    S ≡ I = $50 ✓
    
    
    EXTERNAL FINANCE - Bank Credit (≈30% of investment)
    ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
    
    Firm borrows $30 from bank for investment
         │
         ├─ +$30 Loan
         └─ +$30 Deposit (created simultaneously)
         │
         ↓
    Firm buys capital goods
         │
         ↓
    Supplier receives $30 deposit
         │
         ↓
    Supplier does NOT spend it immediately
         │
         ↓
    = Saving of $30 (convenience lending, non-volitional)
    
    S ≡ I = $30 ✓
    
    
    EXTERNAL FINANCE - Bond/Equity Issue (≈20%)
    ═══════════════════════════════════════════
    
    Firm issues $20 in new bonds
         │
         ↓
    Household buys bonds (abstains from consumption)
         │
         ↓
    Firm invests $20
         │
         ↓
    = Saving of $20 (volitional)
    
    S ≡ I = $20 ✓
    
    ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    TOTAL: I = $100
           S = $50 + $30 + $20 = $100
           
           Volitional:     $20 (20%)
           Non-volitional: $80 (80%)
```

## Deficit Spending & Surplus Spending

```
    ACCOUNTING IDENTITY (Closed economy)
    ═══════════════════════════════════
    
    Total Deficit Spending ≡ Total Surplus Spending
    
    
    Deficit Units:                  Surplus Units:
    ──────────────                  ──────────────
    • Spend > Income                • Spend < Income
    • Borrow to finance gap         • Accumulate assets
    • Mostly: Firms                 • Mostly: Households
    
    
        Firms           Households
        ─────           ──────────
        Spend:  $120    Spend: $80
        Earn:   $100    Earn:  $100
        ─────────────────────────────

      • Bank borrowing (→ +M)         • Deposit accumulation

      • Equity issue                  • Equity purchases
      
      
    ┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │                                                   │
    │  AD grows when:                                  │

    │               → +M (endogenous)                   │
    │                                                   │
