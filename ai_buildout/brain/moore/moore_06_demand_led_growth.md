## Thirlwall's Law (Balance of Payments Constrained Growth)

```
    OPEN ECONOMY CONSTRAINT
    ═══════════════════════
    
    Growth rate limited by:
    
        ẏ_BP = (x/m)·ẏ_world + (1/m)·(ė)
        
    where:
        ẏ_BP = max sustainable growth rate
        x = income elasticity of exports
        m = income elasticity of imports  
        ẏ_world = world income growth
        ė = real exchange rate change
        
        
    Intuition:
    ─────────
    
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │                                                  │
    │  Domestic growth → Imports rise                 │
    │                                                  │
    │  Must be financed by exports                    │
    │  (in long run, can't borrow forever)            │
    │                                                  │
    │  ∴ Domestic growth constrained by               │
    │    ability to export                             │
    │                                                  │
    │  This is DEMAND constraint, not supply!         │
    │    (Foreign demand for our exports)             │
    │                                                  │
    └──────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
    
    
    Policy implication:
    ──────────────────
    
    To grow faster:
    • Increase export competitiveness (tech, quality)
    • Reduce import dependence (develop domestic industry)
    • Devalue currency (raise ė)
    
    NOT: "Increase saving"
    NOT: "Supply-side reforms"
```
