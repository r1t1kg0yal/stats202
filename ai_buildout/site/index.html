The integration problem

Goyal-Harvey-Moore As One Framework

The first phase foregrounds the framework itself. AI is the live case study, but the thesis engine is the integration of accounting closure, investment-cycle dynamics, and collateral/money creation.

01

Moore: The Accounting Substrate

Every AI-capex claim must eventually be located in balance sheets, flows, identities, and model closure.

  • Endogenous money: loans create deposits.
  • Investment creates saving as an identity.
  • Sectoral balances constrain every story.
02

Harvey: The Cycle Engine

Investment is volatile because expectations outrun the realized profit stream that must validate the new capital stock.

  • Animal spirits recover after crisis memories fade.
  • Capex creates profits now and obligations later.
  • Disappointment matters relative to expectations.
03

Goyal: The Money / Collateral Layer

The system expands when collateral and balance-sheet space expand; policy and rates must be read through that collateral engine.

  • Money is collateral and bank balance-sheet space.
  • Inflation is sustained collateral expansion.
  • Rates, QE, deficits, and asset prices can flip signs.

Forward-looking frame

Scenario Matrix

The eventual thesis should not assert one deterministic future. It should map conditional trajectories and the observables that separate them.

Jevons Validation

Inference prices fall, usage explodes, revenues and gross profits rise enough to validate capital.

Validation path

Productivity-Deflation Trap

Real AI services surge, but competitive pass-through and bundling prevent enough nominal profit capture.

Bust path

Platform Rent Capture

A few bottleneck owners capture the surplus through data, distribution, identity, workflow, and cloud rents.

Mixed path

Fiscal / Security Backstop

Government deficits, defense demand, and strategic industrial policy validate part of the buildout.
